BRDD - BUSINESS RULE DRIVEN DESIGN (The Journey)

Working across different scenarios and business areas in the IT world, I acquired knowledge comparing various realities. At the end of the day, I realized that the biggest problems are almost always the same: misunderstandings between the Product/Business team and the Development team; poorly documented projects; and poorly organized legacy systems that only the creators can maintain. Even the use of AI presents difficulties in these contexts, demanding high token expenditure and delaying the performance of new hires.

Aiming to bridge this gap between Business and IT, and to organize the code without inventing a series of complex rules that often do not pay off due to the steep learning curve, I developed a code design pattern based on business rules. This design wasn't born ready. It was refined by multiple brilliant minds across several companies, tested in the trenches of various real-world situations, and adapted based on feedback to feel less "robotic" and more human. It evolved from a rigid idea into a practical, battle-tested standard that actually works in extremely complex scenarios.

Roles

Looking at different functionalities in various systems, they might initially seem completely different from each other, but analyzing them abstractly, the vast majority of features will follow this order:

  • 1- Enrichment of input data if necessary
  • 2- Validation of business rules
  • 3- Execution of the use case
  • 4- Enrichment of the response

Thinking about this, in this model I made it explicit that this separation must be done in different types of services, each with its specialty:

  • BusinessService: Responsible solely for the execution of the main use case action.
  • ValidationService: Responsible for validating business rules.
  • EnrichService: Responsible for enriching data.
  • ClientService: Responsible for interacting with external systems.
  • Listener: Responsible for receiving interactions from external systems.
  • UseCase: Orchestrates other services in case of a more complex feature.

Context

Any validated rule, value set by the system, or action executed beyond the main use case action, must be identified and explicitly attributed to a code that represents its origin in a business context. This origin must have an identification code and must be explicitly documented in the repository.

To ensure that this origin code is present in the codebase, the use of the ValidationContext and ExecutionContext interfaces is recommended. Their function is similar to assert libraries for tests. Later, at the end of the execution, these interfaces serve as a report of everything that was executed during the process, making explicit the origin of that code in the business area.

Conclusion: A Bridge to the Future

BRDD is more than just organization; it is a bridge to the future of software development. By making the code semantically identical to the documentation, we create an environment where AI Agents can act as true partners, understanding the "why" behind every line. This ensures that the system's architecture remains pristine and aligned with business goals as it grows.

BRDD - BUSINESS RULE DRIVEN DESIGN (A Jornada)

Visando eliminar o gap entre o Negócio e a TI, e organizar o código sem a necessidade de inventar regras complexas, desenvolvi um padrão de design de código baseado nas regras de negócio. Este design não nasceu pronto da noite para o dia. Ele foi sendo aprimorado por diversas mentes brilhantes, em várias empresas diferentes, e testado incansavelmente nas mais diversas situações reais. Essa evolução coletiva fez com que o padrão deixasse de ser algo "robótico" ou puramente teórico, tornando-se uma ferramenta prática, humana e validada em cenários de extrema complexidade.

Foco Central

  • 1- Enriquecimento de Dados
  • 2- Validação de Regras de Negócio
  • 3- Execução do Caso de Uso

Serviços

  • UseCase: O orquestrador.
  • BusinessService: Execução da ação principal do caso de uso.
  • ValidationService: Validação de regras.
  • EnrichService: Preparação de dados.

Conclusão: Uma Ponte para o Futuro

O BRDD é mais do que apenas organização; é uma ponte para o futuro do desenvolvimento de software. Ao tornar o código semanticamente idêntico à documentação, criamos um ambiente onde Agentes de IA podem atuar como verdadeiros parceiros, compreendendo o "porquê" por trás de cada linha. Isso garante que a arquitetura do sistema permaneça íntegra e alinhada aos objetivos de negócio à medida que ele cresce.
