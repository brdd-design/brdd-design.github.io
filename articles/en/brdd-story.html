BRDD - BUSINESS RULE DRIVEN DESIGN (The Journey)

Working across various business sectors in the IT world, I realized that structural problems are almost always the same: a chronic misunderstanding between Product/Business teams and Development teams; poorly documented projects; and complex legacy systems that only their creators can maintain.

Aiming to eliminate this gap between Business and IT, and to organize code without creating unnecessary layers of complexity, I developed a design pattern strictly based on business rules. This design was refined by multiple brilliant minds and tested in diverse real-world situations.

Model Foundations

I adopted MVC and DDD as the foundation due to their simplicity and high market adoption. Since this model respects widely accepted standards, it can be adapted to any language or framework.

Roles and Responsibilities

Most features follow a common logical flow:

  1. Enrichment of input data (context preparation);
  2. Validation of business rules (integrity guarantee);
  3. Execution of the business rule (main action);
  4. Enrichment of the response (output formatting).

In the BRDD model, these actions are separated into specialized service types:

  • UseCase: The orchestrator that composes the end-to-end flow.
  • ValidateService: Single-responsibility classes focused on a specific rule.
  • EnrichService: Classes focused on fetching data needed for the context.
  • BusinessService: Responsible for executing the main logic.
  • ClientService: Responsible for communication with external systems.

The Business Context

Any validated rule, value, or side effect must be identified by a code representing its origin in the business context (e.g., R001). This documentation is kept in the repository (e.g., BUSINESS_RULES.md).

Example Mapping:

  • Business Rule: (R001) Validate stock availability.
  • Use Case: (US001) Product consumption.
  • Side Effect: (CE001) External system notification.
  • Loaded Value: (SET001) Operation timestamp.

AI-First Architecture

The great differentiator of BRDD is its native support for AI-assisted development. By making the code semantically identical to the documentation, we create an environment where AI Agents can act as strategic partners.
